# Why was my affiliate application declined?

Getting your affiliate application declined can be frustrating, especially when you're not sure why it happened.

This guide explains common reasons for rejection and how to improve your chances of approval.

Common reasons and how to fix themAffiliate programs carefully review applications to ensure quality partnerships.

Here are the most common reasons for decline:
1.

Incomplete or low-quality websiteCommon issues:Website under construction or showing coming soon pagesInsufficient content (fewer than 10 published articles or posts)Poor user experience or broken functionalityUsing free hosting platforms (like Wix.com URLs)Default WordPress themes with no customizationHow to fix it: Publish at least 10 high-quality pieces of content, customize your site design, and ensure all features work properly before applying.2.

Irrelevant audience or contentCommon issues:Your website content doesn't align with Senja's target marketYour audience isn't interested in testimonial management or marketing toolsNo clear explanation of how you plan to promote SenjaHow to fix it: Ensure your content relates to business tools, marketing, customer reviews, or similar topics.

Clearly explain your promotion strategy in your application.3.

Insufficient traffic or engagementCommon issues:Low website traffic or social media engagementNo recent content updates (site hasn't been updated in months)Limited social proof or community interactionHow to fix it: Build your audience through consistent content creation and engagement before reapplying.4.

Previous violations or negative feedbackCommon issues:History of affiliate program violationsNegative feedback from other affiliate managersUse of prohibited marketing methods (like trademark bidding or spam)How to fix it: Review and improve your marketing practices.

Ensure you understand and follow affiliate program terms and conditions.5.

Application details missing or unclearCommon issues:Not listing all your websites or domainsVague promotion strategy descriptionIncomplete profile informationNon-English applications without English summariesHow to fix it: Provide complete, detailed information about your promotional methods and all relevant websites.

Senja affiliate program requirementsTo qualify for Senja's affiliate program, you should have:An established website or social media presenceContent relevant to business tools, marketing, or customer experienceRegular engagement with your audienceProfessional presentation and user experienceClear explanation of how you plan to promote SenjaMUST ATTEND: Partner Program Onboarding Call (held monthly, invite sent after application)How to appeal or reapplyIf you were already approved previouslyCheck if you're already in the affiliate program by logging into First Promoter.

Some users receive decline notifications even when they're already approved affiliates.

Reapplying after improvementsBefore reapplying:Address the specific issues that likely caused your declineWait at least 30 days between applicationsEnsure your website meets all quality standardsProvide a detailed, professional applicationContacting SenjaIf you believe your application was declined in error or need clarification, contact partners@senja.io.

Provide specific details about your promotional strategy and why you believe you'd be a good fit for the program.

Best practices for future applicationsBefore you applyBuild a substantial content library (minimum 10-15 posts)Establish regular traffic and engagementEnsure your website loads quickly and functions properlyCreate content that demonstrates your understanding of marketing or business toolsDuring applicationList all websites and domains you ownExplain specifically how you'll promote SenjaHighlight your relevant experience and audienceInclude traffic statistics if availableWrite in professional, clear languageAfter approvalOnce approved, you can access your affiliate link and dashboard through FirstPromoter to start promoting and tracking your performance.
